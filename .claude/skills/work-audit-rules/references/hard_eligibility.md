## R200(1) Mandatory Criteria

An applicant for a work permit must meet ALL of the following:

### 1. Valid Job Offer
- Document: Offer letter on employer letterhead
- Requirements:
  - Signed by authorized representative
  - Must be for specific position in Canada
  - Cannot be for self-employment (unless exempt category)
  - Employment contract or letter detailing:
    - Job title
    - Job duties
    - Salary/wages
    - Duration
    - Working conditions
    - Start date

### 2. LMIA or Exemption
- **If LMIA Required**:
  - Service Canada Labour Market Opinion showing positive assessment
  - LMIA valid on date of application
  - Job details match LMIA application

- **If LMIA Exempt**:
  - Employer holds valid International Mobility Program (IMP) position
  - Applicant qualifies for specific category:
    - Canadian-American professional
    - USMCA professional
    - Intra-company transferee
    - Significant Benefits program
    - Free trade agreement category

### 3. Admissibility
- No grounds for inadmissibility under IRPA
- Security: No connections to crime/terrorism
- Criminality: No criminal convictions (or waiver obtained)
- Health: Medical exam completed if required
- Misrepresentation: No false statements

### 4. Temporary Intent
- Clear commitment to leave Canada upon permit expiration
- Cannot have indications of intent to remain permanently
- Factors indicating intent to stay:
  - Spouse/children already in Canada
  - Property purchase or long-term lease
  - Job contract extends beyond permit validity
  - Settlement plan documentation
  - Multiple previous work permits showing pattern

## LMIA-Specific Eligibility

If LMIA required, these additional criteria apply:

### Employer Requirements
- Registered business in Canada
- Labour market opinion issued by Service Canada
- Recruitment undertaken for minimum 4 weeks (national media)
- Proof no Canadian workers could fill position
- Wages meet or exceed:
  - Provincial minimum wage (absolute floor)
  - Prevailing wage for occupation in region
  - Median wage for occupation nationally

### Position Requirements
- Genuine employment opportunity
- Job duties clearly defined
- Skills level matches occupation code (NOC)
- Not displacing Canadian workers
- Temporary need supported by business case

## Exemption Categories

### International Mobility Program (IMP)
- Employer does not require LMIA
- Categories:
  - Significant Benefits (mutual benefit to Canada/employer)
  - Reciprocal employment exchanges
  - Corporate transferees
  - Religious workers
  - Sports/entertainment figures

### Trade Agreement Professionals
- USMCA (formerly NAFTA)
- CPTPP countries
- Canadian-American professionals
- CEPA/bilateral agreements

## Age and Capacity
- Applicant must be 16+ years old
- Must be capable of performing duties
- No mental incapacity issues

## Prerequisite Documents
- Valid passport
- LMIA (if required)
- Job offer letter
- Medical examination (if required)
- Police certificate (if required)
- Educational credentials verification

## Automatic Refusal Grounds

Application will be refused if:

1. **No valid LMIA**
   - Required but not provided
   - Negative assessment
   - Issued to different employer
   - Expired before application

2. **Admissibility Issues**
   - Criminal record not waivable
   - Security concerns
   - Health danger to public
   - Previous misrepresentation

3. **Intent Concerns**
   - Obvious permanent settlement indicators
   - Hidden family/assets in Canada
   - Contradictory statements

4. **Fraudulent Documentation**
   - False employer registration
   - Fraudulent job offer
   - Forged credentials
   - False statements material to decision

## Assessment Officers' Discretion

Even if all R200 criteria met, application can be refused if officer not satisfied:
- Genuineness of job offer
- Legitimacy of employer
- Capability of applicant
- Applicant will leave Canada

This discretion is reviewable by Federal Court for reasonableness.
