## 2025 Policy Changes

### 1. Open Work Permits for Family Members

**Effective Date**: January 21, 2025
**Source**: [IRCC Notice](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/news/notices/changes-open-work-permits-family-members-temporary-residents.html)

#### What Changed
- Restricted eligibility for family open work permits (OWPs)
- Dependent children of foreign workers **no longer eligible**
- New restrictions on spouse eligibility

#### New Requirements for Spouses of International Students
OWPs limited to those whose partners study in:
- Master's programs lasting 16+ months
- Doctoral programs
- Select professional and eligible programs

#### New Requirements for Spouses of Foreign Workers
OWPs require the worker to be employed in:
- TEER 0 or 1 occupations, OR
- Select TEER 2 or 3 positions in sectors:
  - Natural sciences
  - Construction
  - Healthcare
  - Education
  - Other specified sectors

**Additional Requirement**: Worker must have at least **16 months remaining** on their work permit when spouse applies.

#### Exemptions
- Spouses covered by free-trade agreements
- Those transitioning to permanent residence
- Previously approved OWPs remain valid

#### Impact on Audit
- **Risk Badge**: Check `OPEN_SPOUSE` category carefully
- **Document Check**: Verify principal's NOC code and remaining permit duration
- **Cross-reference**: spousal-audit-rules for relationship genuineness

### 2. PGWP Field of Study Requirements

**Effective Date**: November 1, 2024 (initial); June 25, 2025 (major update)
**Source**: [IRCC Field of Study](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/work/after-graduation/eligibility/field-of-study.html)

#### June 25, 2025 Changes
- **119 new fields added** (healthcare, social services, education, trades)
- **178 fields removed**
- Total eligible fields: **920**

#### July 4, 2025 Correction
- IRCC reinstated several fields of study removed on June 25

#### Grandfathering Provisions
Students who applied for study permit **before June 25, 2025**:
- Eligible for PGWP if field was on list at application time
- Even if field has since been removed

Fields removed on June 25, 2025:
- Remain eligible until early 2026 list update
- Applications submitted June 25 - July 4 still qualify

#### Exemptions from Field of Study Requirement
No field of study requirement if:
- PGWP application submitted before November 1, 2024
- Graduated from PGWP-eligible flight school
- Graduated with bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree
- Study permit application before November 1, 2024

#### 2026 Outlook
- IRCC will NOT add or remove eligible fields of study in 2026
- List remains stable

#### Impact on Audit
- **Risk Badge**: `PGWP` category - verify CIP code eligibility
- **Document Check**: Confirm 6-digit CIP code against current list
- **Timing Check**: Application date vs. policy effective dates

## LMIA Policy Status

**Last Page Update**: May 16, 2025
**Source**: [IRCC LMIA Page](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/work-canada/hire-temporary-foreign/find-need-labour-market-impact-assessment.html)

### Current Requirements
- Most employers require LMIA before hiring TFW
- LMIA confirms need for foreign worker
- Verifies no Canadians/PRs available for position

### Exemptions
- International Mobility Program (IMP) exemptions
- Francophone Mobility for bilingual workers outside Quebec
- Free trade agreement categories

### Quebec Specific
- **TFWP**: Workers need Quebec Acceptance Certificate (CAQ)
- **IMP**: CAQ not required
