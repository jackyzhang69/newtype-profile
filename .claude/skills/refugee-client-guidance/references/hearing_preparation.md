# Hearing Preparation Guide

## Before the Hearing

### 2-4 Weeks Before

**Review your claim**:
- Read BOC form multiple times
- Create timeline of key events
- Make list of important names, places, dates
- Practice explaining your story

**Gather documents**:
- Organize all evidence
- Get translations if needed
- Make copies of everything
- Prepare index of documents

**Administrative**:
- Confirm hearing date, time, location
- Arrange transportation
- Confirm interpreter (if requested)
- Confirm representative attendance

### 1 Week Before

**Final preparation**:
- Review documents one more time
- Practice answers to common questions
- Prepare questions you may want to ask
- Review country conditions relevant to your case

**Logistics**:
- Confirm interpreter again
- Know exactly how to get to hearing location
- Plan to arrive 30 minutes early
- Prepare appropriate clothing

### Day Before

**Rest**:
- Get good sleep
- Don't stay up reviewing all night
- Eat properly
- Reduce stress as much as possible

**Prepare materials**:
- Pack documents to bring
- Prepare appropriate clothing
- Set multiple alarms

## At the Hearing

### What to Expect

**Setting**:
- Hearing room (not courtroom)
- IRB member (decision maker)
- Refugee Protection Officer (if present)
- Your representative (if you have one)
- Interpreter (if requested)
- Possibly Minister's representative

**Process**:
1. Introduction and oath/affirmation
2. Preliminary matters
3. Questions about your claim
4. Opportunity to add information
5. Closing (decision may or may not be given)

### How to Testify

**Listen carefully**:
- Let questioner finish before answering
- Make sure you understand the question
- Ask for clarification if needed
- Ask for question to be repeated if necessary

**Answer honestly**:
- Tell the truth - even difficult truths
- If you don't know, say "I don't know"
- If you don't remember, say "I don't remember"
- Don't guess or make up details

**Be consistent**:
- Answers should match your BOC
- If different, explain why (you remembered more, etc.)
- If you made a mistake in BOC, acknowledge it

**Be specific**:
- Give dates when you know them
- Use approximate dates if uncertain ("around March 2024")
- Name people and places
- Describe what happened in detail

**Take your time**:
- Think before answering
- It's okay to pause
- Ask for breaks if needed
- Ask for water if needed

### Common Questions to Prepare For

**Identity**:
- Full name and any other names used
- Date and place of birth
- Nationality and citizenship
- Family members

**Background**:
- Education history
- Employment history
- Religion
- Political involvement

**Persecution**:
- What happened to you?
- Who did it?
- When did it happen?
- How do you know they targeted you because of [Convention ground]?

**Police/Protection**:
- Did you report to police?
- What did they do?
- If not reported, why not?
- Did you seek other protection?

**Country conditions**:
- Is there anywhere safe in your country?
- What would happen if you went to [city]?
- Has the situation changed?

**Flight**:
- When did you decide to leave?
- How did you leave?
- Why Canada?
- Did you claim in other countries?

**Fear**:
- What do you fear if you return?
- Who specifically are you afraid of?
- Why can't you live elsewhere in your country?

### After Questions

You will have opportunity to:
- Add anything you didn't get to say
- Clarify any answers
- Submit additional documents (may need permission)

### Decision

IRB member may:
- Give decision at end of hearing (less common)
- Reserve decision and mail it later (more common)

## After the Hearing

**If accepted**:
- Congratulations!
- You are now a "protected person"
- You can apply for permanent residence
- Follow up steps will be provided

**If rejected**:
- You have right to appeal to RAD (Refugee Appeal Division)
- Strict timelines apply (usually 15 days)
- Speak to representative immediately
- Do not panic - there are options

## Tips for Specific Situations

### If you have trauma

- It's okay to ask for breaks
- Take your time with difficult topics
- If you become upset, it's understandable
- Ask for support if needed

### If using interpreter

- Speak clearly and slowly
- Pause after sentences for interpretation
- If interpretation seems wrong, say so
- Speak in first person ("I went..." not "She says she went...")

### If you made mistakes in BOC

- Acknowledge errors honestly
- Explain why the mistake happened
- Provide correct information now
- Don't try to cover up mistakes
