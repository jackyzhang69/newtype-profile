# Refugee Client Guidance

Client-facing guidance materials for refugee protection claim preparation.

## Document Preparation Guide

### Required Documents

**1. Basis of Claim (BOC) Form**
- Obtain from IRB website: https://irb-cisr.gc.ca/en/forms/pages/rpdspr0201.aspx
- Complete ALL sections - incomplete forms cause delays
- Narrative section is CRITICAL - this is your primary evidence
- Use IRB's Claimant's Guide for help

**2. Identity Documents**
Priority order:
1. Valid passport (all pages, including blank)
2. National ID card
3. Birth certificate
4. Driver's license
5. Any other government-issued ID

If no documents:
- Explain in BOC why documents unavailable
- Provide any alternative evidence (school records, etc.)
- Witness statements from family if possible

**3. Use of Representative (IMM 5476)**
- Required if someone helps you with your claim
- Submit with your application
- Keep a copy for your records

### Supporting Evidence

**Persecution Evidence** (if available):
- Police reports from incidents
- Medical reports showing injuries
- Photographs of injuries or damage
- Threat letters or messages
- News articles about incidents
- Arrest warrants or court documents

**Membership Evidence** (if relevant):
- Political party membership card
- Religious institution letters
- Union membership documents
- Photos at organization events

**Country Conditions** (helpful but IRB has access):
- Human rights reports
- News articles about persecution
- Expert letters if available

## Hearing Preparation Guide

### Before the Hearing

**Know your BOC**:
- Read it multiple times
- Be able to explain every detail
- Remember key dates and names

**Prepare for questions about**:
- Timeline of events
- Specific incidents of persecution
- Why you fear return
- Why you didn't seek protection elsewhere
- Why you chose Canada

**Documents to bring**:
- Copy of your BOC
- All supporting documents
- Identification
- Interpreter request (if needed)

### At the Hearing

**Be honest**: 
- Tell the truth, even if embarrassing
- If you don't know, say "I don't know"
- If you don't remember, say "I don't remember"

**Be consistent**:
- Your testimony should match your BOC
- If different, explain why

**Be specific**:
- Give dates when possible
- Name people and places
- Describe events in detail

**Take your time**:
- Listen to full question
- Think before answering
- Ask for clarification if needed

### Common Questions

**Identity**:
- What is your full name?
- When and where were you born?
- What is your nationality?

**Persecution**:
- Who persecuted you?
- What happened? When?
- Did you report to police? What happened?
- How did you know they were targeting you specifically?

**Convention ground**:
- Why were you targeted?
- Are others in your group targeted?

**State protection**:
- Did you seek help from authorities?
- What did they do?
- Why couldn't they protect you?

**Flight**:
- When did you decide to leave?
- Why Canada?
- How did you get here?

**Return**:
- What do you fear if you return?
- Is there anywhere safe in your country?

## Statement Writing Guide

### Narrative Structure

**Opening**: Who you are
- Name, age, country
- Family background
- Occupation/education

**Middle**: What happened
- In chronological order
- Specific dates when possible
- Who did what to you
- How you felt (fear, trauma)
- Did you seek help? What happened?

**End**: Why you need protection
- Why you can't return
- Why you can't get protection there
- What you fear will happen

### Writing Tips

**Be specific**:
- NOT: "They threatened me many times"
- YES: "On March 5, 2024, two men came to my house at night and said they would kill me if I continued my political activities"

**Include details**:
- Names of people (or descriptions if unknown)
- Dates (or approximate: "early 2024", "around March")
- Places (addresses, landmarks)
- What was said (quote if possible)

**Explain gaps**:
- If you can't remember exact dates, explain why
- If you don't have documents, explain why
- If there's delay in claiming, explain why

**Connect to persecution**:
- Show WHY you were targeted
- Link to your race/religion/politics/etc.

### What NOT to Include

- Exaggerations or embellishments
- Events you didn't personally experience
- Information you can't verify
- Inconsistencies with other documents
