# Refugee Document Classification

## Document Categories

### Tier 1: Critical Documents

These documents are essential for claim eligibility and identity establishment.

| Document | Purpose | Weight | Notes |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Basis of Claim (BOC) | Central claim narrative | CRITICAL | Must be complete |
| Passport | Identity, nationality | CRITICAL | All pages needed |
| Travel document | Entry to Canada | CRITICAL | If no passport |
| National ID | Identity verification | CRITICAL | Country-issued |

### Tier 2: Core Evidence

Supporting evidence for the persecution claim.

| Document | Purpose | Weight | Notes |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Police reports | Persecution incidents | HIGH | Date, response important |
| Medical reports | Injuries from persecution | HIGH | Causation link needed |
| Arrest warrants | Government persecution | HIGH | Verify authenticity |
| Threat documentation | Ongoing risk | HIGH | Source identification |
| Organization membership | Convention ground link | HIGH | Dates of membership |

### Tier 3: Country Conditions

Background evidence establishing persecution risk.

| Document | Purpose | Weight | Notes |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| UNHCR reports | Country conditions | HIGH | Most authoritative |
| Human Rights Watch | Persecution patterns | HIGH | Respected source |
| Amnesty International | Human rights situation | HIGH | Respected source |
| US State Dept | Country assessment | MEDIUM | Official source |
| Expert affidavits | Specific risk analysis | HIGH | Expert credentials |

### Tier 4: Supplementary Evidence

Additional supporting materials.

| Document | Purpose | Weight | Notes |
|----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Photographs | Corroborate events | MEDIUM | Date verification needed |
| News articles | Event corroboration | MEDIUM | Source reliability |
| Witness statements | Third-party confirmation | MEDIUM | Witness credibility |
| Communication records | Threats received | MEDIUM | Authenticity concerns |

## Document Quality Assessment

### High Quality Indicators

- Original or certified copy
- Clear, legible content
- Official stamps/seals intact
- Consistent with other evidence
- From verifiable source
- Recent and relevant

### Quality Concerns

- Photocopies only
- Unclear or partially illegible
- Missing authentication
- Inconsistent with other documents
- Unknown or unverifiable source
- Outdated information

### Potential Authenticity Issues

- Documents appear altered
- Inconsistent fonts/formatting
- Impossible dates or sequences
- Stamps that don't match known formats
- Information conflicts with country knowledge
- Too perfect or template-like

## Classification by Claim Type

### Political Persecution Claims

**Essential**:
- Party membership evidence
- Documentation of political activities
- Evidence of government targeting

**Supporting**:
- News coverage of persecution
- Witness statements from other activists
- Country conditions on political repression

### Religious Persecution Claims

**Essential**:
- Evidence of religious identity
- Documentation of religious activities
- Evidence of religious restrictions

**Supporting**:
- Religious institution confirmation
- Conversion documentation (if applicable)
- Country conditions on religious minorities

### Gender-Based Claims

**Essential**:
- Evidence of gender-based harm
- Medical/psychological reports
- Country conditions for women

**Supporting**:
- Expert evidence on gender persecution
- Support organization documentation
- Comparable cases from country

### LGBTQ+ Claims

**Essential**:
- Evidence of sexual orientation/gender identity
- Documentation of persecution
- Country conditions for LGBTQ+ persons

**Supporting**:
- Relationship evidence
- Community membership
- Expert testimony on country treatment

## Document Handling Protocol

### On Receipt

1. Create inventory of all documents
2. Note original vs. copy status
3. Check for translation needs
4. Identify authenticity concerns
5. Cross-reference with BOC claims

### Analysis Phase

1. Extract key information per schema
2. Build timeline from documents
3. Identify consistency/inconsistency
4. Note missing expected documents
5. Assess overall evidence strength

### Report Generation

1. Summarize each document category
2. Highlight strongest evidence
3. Note gaps or concerns
4. Link evidence to legal requirements
5. Provide overall assessment
