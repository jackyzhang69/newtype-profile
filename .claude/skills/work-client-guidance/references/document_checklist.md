# Work Permit Document Checklist

Complete checklist of documents needed for a strong work permit application.

## Baseline Documents (REQUIRED)

These documents are mandatory for any work permit application:

### 1. Valid Passport
- [ ] Valid for duration of intended stay
- [ ] Copies of all pages (biographical and visa pages)
- [ ] Original available for verification
- [ ] Minimum 6 months validity remaining

**How to obtain**: Renew through your home country's passport office if expired

### 2. Job Offer Letter
- [ ] Original signed by employer's authorized representative
- [ ] On official employer letterhead
- [ ] Clearly stating: job title, duties, start date, salary, location
- [ ] Specific dates (not generic template)
- [ ] Employer contact information included

**How to obtain**: Request from employer; ensure it's original signed letter

### 3. Labour Market Opinion (LMIA) - If Required
- [ ] Original copy from Service Canada
- [ ] Shows POSITIVE assessment
- [ ] File number clearly visible
- [ ] Validity period not expired
- [ ] Employer and position match job offer exactly

**How to obtain**: Employer obtains from Service Canada; allow 2-4 months

**If LMIA Exempt**:
- [ ] Documentation proving exemption (TN, P-1, IMP, etc.)
- [ ] Employer authorization letter if applicable

### 4. Educational Credentials
- [ ] Official degree/diploma certificate
- [ ] Transcript of marks/grades (if available)
- [ ] Translation to English (if issued in other language)
- [ ] Credential evaluation if from non-English institution (WES or EVA)

**How to obtain**:
- Contact university/college for official documents
- Use WES or EVA for international credential evaluation (8-12 weeks)

### 5. Work Experience Documentation
- [ ] Reference letters from previous employers (minimum 2)
- [ ] Letters should include: dates of employment, job title, duties, recommendation
- [ ] Contact information for references
- [ ] If available: employment contracts or pay stubs

**How to obtain**: Request from previous employers or supervisors

## Supporting Documents (CONDITIONAL)

Required based on your circumstances:

### Medical Examination (Required if from certain countries)
- [ ] IMM 1017 form completed by panel physician
- [ ] Must be done within 12 months of application
- [ ] Result shows medical admissibility (approved)

**How to obtain**: Schedule with IRCC-approved panel physician

**Cost**: Approximately $300-500 CAD

### Police Certificate
- [ ] Issued by police/security authority in each country of residence 6+ months
- [ ] Shows result: CLEAR or acceptable
- [ ] Dated within 6 months of application (preferred)
- [ ] Original or certified copy

**How to obtain**: Contact police authority in each country

**Timeline**: 2-8 weeks depending on country

### Financial Documents
- [ ] Bank statements (last 6 months)
- [ ] Tax returns (last 2-3 years)
- [ ] Investment account statements
- [ ] Proof of savings or financial capacity

**Purpose**: Show you can support yourself in Canada

## Strategic Documents (RECOMMENDED)

These strengthen your application significantly:

### Educational Credential Verification
- [ ] World Education Services (WES) assessment
- [ ] Educational Credentials Evaluation (EVA)
- [ ] Direct university confirmation letter
- [ ] Professional certification verification if applicable

**Why**: Proves educational credentials are genuine and accredited

**Cost**: $200-300 CAD

### Professional Reference Letters
- [ ] From previous supervisors/managers
- [ ] Describes your work performance and capabilities
- [ ] Confirms you are qualified for the new position
- [ ] From different employers (shows track record)

**Why**: Corroborates your work experience and capability

### Employment Contract
- [ ] Detailed employment agreement with employer
- [ ] Specifies job duties, salary, benefits, duration
- [ ] Shows employment relationship is genuine and substantive

**Why**: Demonstrates genuine position and confirms job details

### Employer Company Information
- [ ] Business registration or corporate documents
- [ ] Financial statements (if publicly available)
- [ ] Number of employees
- [ ] Years in operation
- [ ] Industry information

**Why**: Establishes employer legitimacy

### Language Test Results (If Required)
- [ ] IELTS, TOEFL, or TEF results
- [ ] Taken within 2 years
- [ ] Scores meet position requirements

**Why**: Demonstrates language capability if position requires it

### Certificates of Good Standing
- [ ] Professional license verification (if applicable)
- [ ] Professional association membership (if relevant)
- [ ] Certification in relevant field

**Why**: Demonstrates professional credentials and standing

## Ties to Home Country Documents

Document your intention to return home:

### Family Ties
- [ ] Marriage certificate (if applicable)
- [ ] Birth certificates of children
- [ ] Letters from family members
- [ ] Family photos

### Property/Asset Documentation
- [ ] Property deed or ownership certificate
- [ ] Vehicle registration
- [ ] Investment account statements
- [ ] Bank account statements showing home country accounts

### Employment Ties
- [ ] Letter from home country employer confirming leave of absence
- [ ] Letter confirming position awaits upon return
- [ ] Professional license in home country
- [ ] Business ownership documents if applicable

### Personal Ties
- [ ] Community involvement documentation
- [ ] Proof of membership in organizations
- [ ] Religious or cultural affiliations
- [ ] Educational institution connections

## Document Preparation Tips

### Originals vs. Copies
- **Originals**: Best for critical documents (passport, LMIA, job offer)
- **Certified Copies**: Acceptable if original unavailable; must be certified by authorized person
- **Regular Copies**: Not acceptable for primary documents

### Language & Translation
- **English/French Documents**: Submit as provided
- **Other Languages**: Include certified English translation
- **Translator Requirements**: Translator must sign and certify accuracy

### Organization
- **Folder Structure**:
  - Folder 1: Baseline documents
  - Folder 2: Conditional documents
  - Folder 3: Strategic documents
  - Folder 4: Ties documentation
- **Labeling**: Clearly label each document with name and type
- **Indexing**: Create table of contents if many documents

### Quality Standards
- **Scanning**: Use high-quality scanner (300 DPI)
- **Legibility**: All documents must be clearly readable
- **Completeness**: Include all pages of multi-page documents
- **Professional Appearance**: Clean, organized, professional presentation

## Timeline Checklist

### Months 6-4 Before Applying
- [ ] Request job offer from employer
- [ ] Employer begins LMIA process (2-4 months)
- [ ] Gather educational credentials
- [ ] Request reference letters from employers

### Months 4-2 Before Applying
- [ ] LMIA decision received from Service Canada
- [ ] Submit credentials for WES/EVA evaluation
- [ ] Book medical examination (if required)
- [ ] Order police certificate
- [ ] Gather financial documents

### Months 2-1 Before Applying
- [ ] Receive credential evaluation
- [ ] Receive medical examination results
- [ ] Receive police certificate
- [ ] Prepare employer company information
- [ ] Organize all documents

### Week Before Applying
- [ ] Review checklist one final time
- [ ] Verify all dates and information
- [ ] Scan or copy all documents
- [ ] Create organized document package

## Document Verification

### Before Submitting, Verify:
- [ ] All documents are complete (no missing pages)
- [ ] All information is accurate and consistent
- [ ] Signatures and dates are present and legible
- [ ] No alterations or white-out visible
- [ ] Language requirements met (English/French or translated)
- [ ] Professional quality and presentation
- [ ] Organized logically for reviewer

## Common Document Issues to Avoid

### ❌ Do NOT Submit:
- Unsigned job offer or LMIA
- Job offer from unknown/unverifiable employer
- Educational credentials from unaccredited institution
- Medical exam more than 12 months old
- Police certificate more than 6 months old (if possible)
- Photocopies of important documents (unless certified)
- Documents with alterations or white-out
- Generic template letters lacking specific information
- Incomplete documents with missing pages

### ✅ Do Submit:
- Original signed documents where possible
- Complete documents with all pages
- Professional quality copies
- Current credentials and examinations
- Employer verification documents
- Detailed reference letters
- Comprehensive tie documentation
