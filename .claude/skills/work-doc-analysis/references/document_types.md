# Work Permit Document Types

## Baseline Documents (Required)

### 1. Valid Passport
**Purpose**: Identity verification and travel authorization
**Format**: Physical or certified copy
**Key Information**:
- Full name and date of birth
- Passport number and country
- Issue and expiry dates
- Travel history (visas and stamps)

**What to validate**:
- Passport must be valid for duration of work permit
- No alterations or damage
- All pages present and legible
- Previous visas showing travel patterns

**Red flags**:
- Expired or expiring within 6 months
- Damage or alterations
- Visa refusals visible
- Multiple passport numbers (suggest lost/replaced)

### 2. Job Offer Letter
**Purpose**: Employment verification and position details
**Format**: Original signed letter on employer letterhead
**Key Information**:
- Employer legal name and contact
- Specific job title and duties
- Start date and duration
- Salary and benefits
- Authorized signature

**What to validate**:
- Unsigned and original (not photocopy)
- Duties match job requirements for position
- Salary reasonable for position
- Employer can be contacted and verified

**Red flags**:
- Unsigned or undated
- Generic duties description
- No start date specified
- Wages significantly below market
- Employer contact information missing
- Inconsistencies with LMIA

### 3. LMIA (Labour Market Opinion)
**Purpose**: Labour market verification (if required)
**Format**: Official Service Canada document
**Key Information**:
- Service Canada file number
- Employer name and location
- Position NOC code and title
- Positive assessment date
- Validity period

**What to validate**:
- LMIA must show Positive assessment
- Employer name matches job offer exactly
- Position title aligns with job offer
- LMIA must not be expired
- Salary in LMIA aligns with offer

**Red flags**:
- Negative assessment
- Different employer name
- Expired LMIA
- Position title mismatch
- Lower salary in LMIA than offer
- Conditions not met by applicant

### 4. Educational Credentials
**Purpose**: Qualification verification for position requirements
**Format**: Official document or verified copy
**Key Information**:
- Institution name and location
- Degree type (Bachelor, Master, Diploma, etc.)
- Field of study
- Graduation date
- Grade or GPA if relevant

**What to validate**:
- Institution is recognized and accredited
- Graduation date is authentic
- Credential matches job requirements
- Field of study relevant to position
- Any required certifications are current

**Red flags**:
- Unaccredited institution
- Vague degree name
- No graduation date
- Credential from diploma mill
- Credential not relevant to position
- Certificate expired (if applicable)

### 5. Work Experience References
**Purpose**: Employment history and capability verification
**Format**: Reference letter from employer or supervisor
**Key Information**:
- Employer name and position
- Employment dates
- Job title and key responsibilities
- Relevance to current position
- Reference contact information

**What to validate**:
- Reference person can be contacted
- Employment dates are verifiable
- Duties match job requirements
- Experience level appropriate for position
- No gaps in employment timeline

**Red flags**:
- Reference contact unreachable
- Vague duties description
- Dates don't align with other documents
- No indication of how this prepares for role
- Generic template letter
- Reference is family member (bias)

## Conditional Documents (Sometimes Required)

### 6. Medical Examination (IMM 1017)
**Purpose**: Health and security verification
**Format**: Official government medical form
**Key Information**:
- Examination date (within 12 months)
- Examining physician name
- Medical findings
- Vaccination status
- Panel physician location

**When required**:
- Applicant from certain countries
- Applicant has lived outside origin country
- Work in food/healthcare industry
- Applicant has significant health history

**What to validate**:
- Conducted by panel physician
- Within 12-month validity period
- All sections completed
- Result is APPROVED or acceptable

**Red flags**:
- Examination more than 12 months old
- Incomplete sections
- Medical conditions that may render inadmissible
- Vaccination record gaps

### 7. Police Certificate
**Purpose**: Security and criminality verification
**Format**: Official government document
**Key Information**:
- Issuing country and date
- Applicant identification
- Clearance result (passed/failed/conditions)
- Any criminal record noted

**When required**:
- Applicant age 18+
- Residence of 6+ months in any country
- Previous criminal convictions

**What to validate**:
- Issued by official government body
- Current or within acceptable validity period
- Result is CLEAR or acceptable explanation
- Any noted issues are not prohibitive

**Red flags**:
- Certificate more than 3-6 months old
- Issued by non-official entity
- Criminal record or concerning findings
- Missing explanation for noted issues

## Supporting Documents (Strengthen Application)

### 8. Employment Contract
**Purpose**: Clarifies and memorializes employment terms
**Format**: Signed agreement between employer and applicant
**Key Information**:
- Detailed job duties and responsibilities
- Employment duration and terms
- Compensation and benefits
- Workplace location and hours
- Any special conditions

**Strategic value**:
- Demonstrates genuineness of position
- Clarifies terms beyond offer letter
- Shows employee commitment
- Can address wage/conditions concerns

### 9. Employer Company Information
**Purpose**: Establishes employer legitimacy and business need
**Format**: Business registration, financial statements, company profile
**Key Information**:
- Legal business registration
- Years in operation
- Number of employees
- Industry and business description
- Financial stability

**Strategic value**:
- Proves employer is legitimate business
- Demonstrates genuine business need for position
- Shows employer stability
- Addresses fraud concerns

### 10. Credential Verification Letters
**Purpose**: Third-party confirmation of educational credentials
**Format**: Letters from WES (World Education Services), EVA, or university
**Key Information**:
- Institution confirmed
- Degree/credential confirmed
- Equivalency (if applicable)
- GPA or grades
- Verification date

**Strategic value**:
- Addresses credential authenticity concerns
- Provides professional verification
- Shows education legitimacy
- Demonstrates due diligence by applicant

### 11. Professional Reference Letters
**Purpose**: Corroborates experience and capability claims
**Format**: Letters from former supervisors or colleagues
**Key Information**:
- Employer/supervisor name and contact
- Dates of employment
- Job duties and accomplishments
- Recommendation for position
- Professional assessment

**Strategic value**:
- Confirms work experience claims
- Speaks to capability and professionalism
- Demonstrates industry connections
- Can address credential gaps

### 12. Financial Documentation
**Purpose**: Shows financial capacity to support work permit
**Format**: Bank statements, tax returns, payslips
**Key Information**:
- Recent bank statements (3-6 months)
- Tax returns showing income
- Investment accounts
- Property ownership

**Strategic value**:
- Demonstrates ties to home country
- Shows financial stability
- Can address ties/intent concerns
- Proves previous employment

## Strategic Documents (Address Specific Concerns)

### 13. Return Plan Statement
**Purpose**: Demonstrates intent to leave Canada after permit expires
**Format**: Personal statement from applicant
**Key Information**:
- Plans upon permit expiration
- Family/property awaiting at home
- Career plans after Canada
- Timeline for return
- Bindings to home country

**When strategic**:
- Immigrant intent concerns
- Weak ties to home country
- Multiple previous work permits

### 14. Family Ties Documentation
**Purpose**: Demonstrates connections to home country
**Format**: Birth certificates, marriage certificates, family photos
**Key Information**:
- Spouse/children names and locations
- Parents/siblings names and locations
- Marriage certificate
- Birth certificates
- Family photos and correspondence

**When strategic**:
- Immigrant intent concerns
- No ties identified
- Sole support for family abroad

### 15. Property/Asset Documentation
**Purpose**: Shows financial ties to home country
**Format**: Property deeds, vehicle registration, investment statements
**Key Information**:
- Property address and value
- Year purchased
- Outstanding mortgages
- Vehicle/asset ownership
- Investment accounts

**When strategic**:
- Minimal ties concerns
- Questions about return plan
- Demonstrating substantial assets abroad

### 16. Employment History in Home Country
**Purpose**: Establishes job to return to
**Format**: Employment letter or business documentation
**Key Information**:
- Current or recent employment
- Job title and organization
- Leave arrangements
- Return date commitment
- Career progression

**When strategic**:
- Immigrant intent concerns
- No employment at origin
- Questions about return arrangements

## Document Quality Standards

### Original vs. Copy
- **Original**: Preferred for all documents
- **Certified Copy**: Acceptable if original unavailable; must be certified by authorized person
- **Photocopy**: Generally not acceptable unless certified

### Language
- **English/French**: Submit as provided
- **Other Language**: Must include certified English translation
- **Translation**: Translator must sign and certify accuracy

### Authenticity Verification
- Official seals/stamps present
- Consistent with institution/issuer style
- Document format appropriate to era
- No alterations or white-out
- Professional reproduction quality

## Document Organization for Application

### Recommended Submission Order
1. Cover letter explaining application
2. Job offer letter
3. LMIA (if applicable)
4. Educational credentials
5. Work experience letters
6. Passport copy
7. Medical examination results (if completed)
8. Police certificate (if applicable)
9. Supporting documents organized by category
10. Any strategic documents addressing concerns
