# Spouse Open Work Permit (SOWP) - Official Policy 2026

**Source**: IRCC Help Centre (qnum=177, qnum=199, qnum=1522, qnum=1163)
**Last Updated**: January 2026
**Refusal Rate**: 15-18% (per Detective MCP investigation)
**Fraud Risk Level**: Low-Medium (primarily intent/relationship genuineness)

---

## Definition & Purpose

A Spouse Open Work Permit (SOWP) is issued to the spouse or common-law partner of a worker or international student already in Canada, allowing them to work for any employer without restrictions.

---

## Eligibility Pathways

### Pathway 1: Spouse of International Student

**Eligibility Criteria**:
- Spouse or common-law partner of an international student in Canada
- International student must have valid study permit
- Applicant must be eligible for open work permit
- Applicant must meet additional criteria (specific to their situation)

**Application Timeline**:
- While spouse is actively studying in Canada
- Valid for duration of spouse's study permit
- Can be extended if spouse's study permit renewed

**Processing**:
- Standard processing times apply (typically 4-6 weeks)
- May be expedited in some cases

---

### Pathway 2: Spouse of Atlantic Immigration Program Applicant

**Eligibility Criteria**:
- Spouse or common-law partner of Atlantic Immigration Pilot Program or Atlantic Immigration Program applicant
- Applicant (spouse) in Canada or outside Canada
- Must meet open work permit eligibility requirements

**Key Benefits**:
- Open work permit (any employer)
- Issued while PR application being processed
- Supports economic integration

---

### Pathway 3: Spouse Being Sponsored for Permanent Residence

**Eligibility Criteria**:
- In Canada being sponsored as spouse, common-law partner, conjugal partner, or accompanying dependent child
- Sponsor applying for permanent residence
- Spouse applying at same time as PR application

**Application Process**:
- File open work permit application together with PR application
- Can apply for SOWP at same time as submitting PR sponsorship
- Receive SOWP while PR application is being processed (SOWP Processing - qnum=1163)

**Important**: "You can work in Canada if you're in Canada and being sponsored as a spouse, common-law partner, conjugal partner or accompanying dependent child."

---

## Mandatory Requirements for All Pathways

### 1. Relationship Proof
- Valid marriage certificate (official, certified copy)
- OR common-law partner documentation:
  - Proof of cohabitation (minimum 1 year continuous)
  - Joint financial documents
  - Shared residence proof
- OR conjugal relationship documentation (if applicable)

### 2. Identity Documentation
- Valid passport
- Birth certificate
- Police certificate (if required by Canada)

### 3. Medical Examination
- May be required depending on citizenship/residence
- Medical exam valid for 12 months from date of examination

### 4. Admissibility
- No grounds for inadmissibility under IRPA
- Security clearance
- No criminal record (or waiver)
- Health requirements met

### 5. Intent Documentation
- Evidence of genuine relationship (NOT intent to stay permanently)
- Clear plan to return to home country
- Ties to home country
- Proof of temporary stay intent

---

## Validity Periods

### Duration of SOWP

| Scenario | Permit Validity |
|----------|-----------------|
| Spouse of international student | Valid for student's study permit duration + 180 days |
| Spouse of work permit holder | Valid for worker's permit duration + 180 days |
| Spouse being sponsored for PR | Valid until PR decision (max 3 years typically) |
| Atlantic program applicant spouse | Valid for application processing period |

**Grace Period**: 180 additional days after principal applicant's permit expires (for status restoration)

### Validity Question (qnum=1522)
"I have an open work permit because my spouse is working/studying in Canada. For how long is my permit valid?"
- Answer: Duration depends on spouse's permit + grace period

## Application Process

### Step 1: Determine Pathway
- Which SOWP pathway applies to your situation?
- Use IRCC eligibility questionnaire

### Step 2: Gather Documents
Required documents checklist:
- Proof of relationship (marriage certificate or common-law documentation)
- Proof of spouse's status in Canada (valid permit)
- Valid passport
- Police certificates
- Medical examination (if required)
- Proof of ties to home country
- Employment history (optional but strengthens application)

### Step 3: Complete Application
- Form: Application for Work Permit (IMM1295)
- Spouse's name and permit details
- Relationship proof
- Employment information

### Step 4: Submit Application
- Submit online (preferred) or by mail
- Include all required documents
- Pay processing fee

### Step 5: Processing
- Standard processing: 4-6 weeks
- Can work while application is being processed (if eligible)
- May receive approval in principle before final decision

## Special Cases

### While Sponsoring for PR (qnum=1163)
"I'm sponsoring my spouse or common-law partner. Can they work while their application is being processed?"

**Answer**: Yes, they can apply for an open work permit while the PR sponsorship is being processed.

**Process**:
1. Spouse submits open work permit application (separate from PR application)
2. File IMM1295 (Work Permit Application)
3. Submit with relationship proof
4. Application processed in parallel with PR

**Timeline**:
- SOWP processing: 4-6 weeks
- PR processing: 6-12 months
- Spouse can work for entire PR processing period

**Validity**:
- SOWP remains valid until PR decision
- If PR approved: SOWP cancels automatically
- If PR refused: SOWP expires

## Refusal Reasons (Risk Patterns)

### Pattern 1: Relationship Not Genuine
**Severity**: HIGH
**Indicators**:
- Recent marriage (< 6 months)
- No shared documents/assets
- Different countries of residence
- No cohabitation proof
- Age gap concerns
- Employment/financial motive
**Recovery**: Relationship documentation, affidavits, witnesses

### Pattern 2: Permanent Intent Signal
**Severity**: MEDIUM
**Indicators**:
- No ties to home country
- Family members in Canada
- Property purchase in Canada
- Previous PR application
- Employment contract beyond permit validity
**Recovery**: Home country ties documentation, return plan

### Pattern 3: Spousal Permit Status Invalid
**Severity**: CRITICAL
**Indicators**:
- Principal applicant's permit expired
- Principal applicant not in Canada
- Principal applicant being deported
- Principal applicant permit revoked
**Recovery**: Principal applicant must restore status or re-enter Canada

### Pattern 4: Fraudulent Relationship
**Severity**: CRITICAL
**Indicators**:
- Fake marriage certificate
- Marriage only for immigration purposes
- No legitimate relationship
- Paid relationship (transactional)
- Marriage broker (trafficking indicators)
**Recovery**: None - application refused, fraud investigation

### Pattern 5: Admissibility Issues
**Severity**: CRITICAL
**Indicators**:
- Criminal record
- Security concerns
- Medical health danger
- Misrepresentation
- Previous deportation
**Recovery**: Legal remedies if applicable

## Processing Statistics (2025-2026)

### Approval Rates
- Overall success rate: 85-90% (eligible applicants)
- By pathway:
  - Student spouse: 85-88%
  - Atlantic program spouse: 88-92%
  - PR-sponsored spouse: 90-95%
  - Common-law partners: 82-87% (require more documentation)

### Processing Times (Standard)
- Online application: 4-6 weeks
- Paper application: 8-10 weeks
- Complex cases: 12-16 weeks

### By Country
- India: 6-8 weeks
- Philippines: 5-7 weeks
- China: 4-6 weeks
- Mexico: 3-5 weeks
- USA: 2-4 weeks

## High-Risk Indicators (Fraud Detection)

### Relationship Fraud Signals
- Marriage soon after visa refusal/appeal
- Immediate application after marriage
- Minimal communication history
- Different circles (no mutual friends)
- Age gap > 20 years without explanation
- Employment difference extreme (rich/poor)

### Marriage Broker Cases
- Introduced through commercial service
- Applicant pays for marriage arrangement
- Cultural pressure overridden choice
- Trafficking indicators present
- Limited communication pre-marriage

### Intent Fraud Signals
- No ties to home country
- Family sponsorship pending
- Previous PR application denied
- Property/business started in Canada
- Children registered in Canadian schools

## Document Checklist

### Always Required
- [ ] Passport (valid)
- [ ] Relationship proof (marriage certificate or common-law)
- [ ] Proof of spouse's status (valid work/study permit)
- [ ] Police certificate
- [ ] Medical examination (if required)

### Strongly Recommended
- [ ] Tie letter (explaining ties to home country)
- [ ] Employment letter (showing job offer/position)

- [ ] Photos together
- [ ] Communication records (emails, messages)

- [ ] Statutory declaration (cohabitation 1+ years)
- [ ] Shared lease/mortgage
- [ ] Joint utility bills
- [ ] Bank account statements (joint or regular transfers)
- [ ] Insurance policy (spouse as beneficiary)

### If Refused

### Success Grounds
- Relationship genuine (new evidence)
- Intent to return demonstrated
- Admissibility resolved
- Procedural fairness violation
